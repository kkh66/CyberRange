Understanding Active Directory and Remote Desktop

A Simple Guide to Key IT Concepts

What is Active Directory (AD)?

Active Directory (AD) is a system used by companies to organize and manage the information about their network. It's like a huge address book that tracks users, computers, printers, and other devices in the company.

AD decides who has access to what on the company's network. For example, when you log into your work computer, AD checks your username and password to make sure you are allowed to use it and access files or applications.

In short: Active Directory controls access, security, and organization in a company's network, ensuring only authorized users can do certain things.

Why is Active Directory Often Targeted by Hackers?

Active Directory is a major target for hackers because it controls access to everything in the network. If a hacker gains control over AD, they can access files, steal data, and even take down systems.

Why do hackers target AD?

  • It’s the key to everything: AD controls access to all systems, files, and services.
  • Admin access: The people managing AD have powerful access to the whole network, making it a prime target for hackers.
  • Gateway to other systems: AD can be used to access other parts of the network.
  • High-value credentials: AD stores usernames, passwords, and other sensitive data that hackers can steal to impersonate users.

In short: AD is like the central control hub for everything on a company's network, making it a very attractive target for cybercriminals.

How Does Active Directory Authentication Work?

When you try to log into a computer on a network, AD checks your identity to make sure you're authorized to use it. Here's a simplified breakdown:

  1. Step 1: You type in your username and password.
  2. Step 2: The computer asks AD to verify your credentials.
  3. Step 3: AD checks the password and either grants or denies access based on a match.

There are two main methods of authentication:

  • Kerberos: This method gives you a special pass or "ticket" to access systems, like a school ID that proves you're allowed in.
  • NTLM: An older method where your password is checked each time you log in or access something.

Important Note: If someone steals your password or your authentication ticket, they might impersonate you to access sensitive systems and data.

What is RDP (Remote Desktop Protocol)?

RDP is a technology that allows you to access and control another computer remotely over the internet or a network. It’s like being able to sit at your desk at home but still use your work computer in the office.

For example, if you're working from home and need a program that’s only on your office computer, you can use RDP to connect and work on it remotely.

Key point: RDP allows you to control another computer as if you were sitting right in front of it.

Why is RDP Often Targeted by Hackers?

RDP is a common target for hackers because it allows remote access to systems. If an attacker gains access to an RDP session, they can take control of the target computer and its data.

  • Weak Passwords: Many RDP attacks come from weak passwords that are easy for hackers to guess.
  • Exposed RDP Ports: Sometimes companies leave RDP ports open to the internet, making it easy for attackers to find and exploit.
  • Unpatched Systems: If the RDP software is not kept up to date, vulnerabilities can be exploited.

Quick RDP Summary: RDP allows you to remotely access computers, but if not properly secured, it can be exploited by attackers to gain unauthorized access to systems and data.
